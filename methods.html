Get it together

After you have your inspo and materials, you could just follow an existing pattern and run your project through a sewing machine. But what if you went the extra mile and did it ALL historically accurately? Well, unless you chose inspo from the late 18th century on, they didn’t have sewing machines. That’s right…it’s time for a hand muscle marathon!

The pattern

You can’t sew a bunch of rectangles or triangles together and hope for the best…right? Actually, if you’re going for a look from the Middle Ages or earlier, you could have luck with just that! You can get your basic measurements from a T-shirt or simple dress that fits you well, the length of skirt you’d want, and the approximate circumference of the skirt. Common stitches

Once the pattern is figured out, our next step is to begin sewing! Most stitches have remained the same throughout time, but to give you a good idea of what would’ve been used, here are some examples:

  • Running stitch – this is good for basting pieces together while figuring out the exact pattern and fit you need.
  • Backstitch – this is a strong stitch that is great for seams.
  • Whipstitch – this would be used for hems, holding down seam allowance, and finishing raw edges.

These are fairly easy stitches to learn and can be very versatile. They are by no means the only options available, but they can get you started and will most likely get you through your first project.

Happy Sewing!
